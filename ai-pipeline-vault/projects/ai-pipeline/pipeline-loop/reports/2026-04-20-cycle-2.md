# Pipeline Cycle Report — cycle 2 (2026-04-20)

## Summary

Cycle 1's big wins landed: BEC-34 (promotion) gives all 10 clusters a registered champion, predictions now cover 500 symbols (was 218), and BEC-35 (portfolio) produces properly sector-diversified allocations (4-5 sectors per profile, no 100%-ConsumerCyclical anymore). BEC-37 (isotonic calibration) also works spectacularly where it ran — ECE drops from 0.15-0.22 to ~0.00 and ConsumerCyclical prob_up mean fell from 0.90 to 0.52. However three concrete regressions / incomplete fixes remain: (1) BEC-36 normalization shows no change — 122/191 features still have std<0.5, so that fix either landed incorrectly or requires Stage 0 re-run (features_normalized.parquet mtime 04-16); (2) the Utilities/RealEstate/FinancialServices champions are still the pre-BEC-37/BEC-38 runs from 04-20T01:xx (before those PRs merged at noon), so isotonic never fired for them and the override's `feature_mask_rate=0.15` isn't reflected — BEC-38 hasn't actually been tested for the clusters it targets; (3) backtest info ratios are negative across all 9 (profile × regime) cells, portfolios underperform SPY.

Worst offenders still include `km_returnonassets`, `fr_inventoryturnover`, `km_netdebttoebitda_qoq` (std≈0 — these fundamentals never get meaningful Z-score because their distributions are near-degenerate even after clipping). **BEC-36 needs verification**: either force-rerun `make normalize` (and downstream) on the current run, or acknowledge that for ~122 fundamentals the clipping+z-score approach fundamentally cannot produce unit variance (a rank/quantile transform would be needed).

## Verdict & reasoning

**Verdict: improve.**

Stage 0 has a concrete verifiable regression (normalization stats didn't change after BEC-36 merged — either fix didn't land or the clipping-first approach fundamentally cannot standardize 122 near-degenerate fundamentals). Stage 2 has a concrete operational bug: 3/10 champions predate BEC-37 and BEC-38 so those fixes are untested on their target clusters, and the pipeline keeps selecting the older pre-fix runs. Stage 5 has a likely cap violation (conservative profile has 30% in CCI but CLAUDE.md caps conservative at 20%). Stage 6 info ratios are uniformly negative — portfolios consistently underperform SPY. These are all concrete and implementable with plausible impact stories.

## Suggested improvements (if verdict=improve)

1. **Force re-training of pre-noon champions so BEC-37/BEC-38 actually take effect** — Stage 2/3. The Utilities/RealEstate/FinancialServices champions are still the 04-20T01:xx runs that predate the isotonic and per-cluster-overrides merges. Either (a) delete the pre-noon runs for those 3 clusters from MLflow and re-run `make train-clusters promote`, or (b) tighten the generalization-adjusted champion score so that a run without isotonic calibration (missing `isotonic_fitted` metric) is strictly dispreferred vs a run with it, falling back to the newer run. Expected impact: the 3 over-confident / overfit clusters finally get their targeted treatment and we can empirically verify BEC-38 works.

2. **Audit and fix conservative-profile sector cap** — Stage 5. `data/portfolios.parquet` shows Conservative with CCI at 30% total weight, while CLAUDE.md and config spec cap conservative at `max_sector_weight=0.20`. Walk through `src/portfolio/optimizer.py` to verify the cap is profile-specific (not inheriting aggressive's 30%) and that BEC-35's per-sector top-K selection doesn't bypass the cap by pre-loading too many CCI candidates. Expected impact: Conservative profile respects its own risk budget; prevents a single sector from dominating >1/5 of the "capital preservation" portfolio.

3. **Verify/rerun BEC-36 normalization fix end-to-end** — Stage 0. `features_normalized.parquet` has mtime 2026-04-16 (skipped by `make pipeline`'s "clusters exist" early-exit) and still has 122/191 features with std<0.5. Either force `make normalize` to always rerun when `normalization_stats.json` is older than a threshold, or drop a `.renormalize` sentinel flag to force the step. Separately, for the ~50 fundamentals with std≈0 even after clipping (e.g., `km_returnonassets`, `fr_inventoryturnover`), switch to rank/quantile-normal transform for features flagged as "near-degenerate" in the stats. Expected impact: LSTM finally gets unit-variance input on all features (not just technicals), modest lift to val precision.

4. **Reduce train-val accuracy gap via stronger regularization on the 6 remaining wide-gap clusters** — Stage 2. BEC-38 only targets Util/RE/FS, but Miscellaneous now has +0.31 train-val gap, ConsumerCyclical +0.20, CommunicationServices-Industrials +0.17, Healthcare +0.14, BasicMaterials +0.16. Extend the per-cluster overrides (lower `max_overfit_gap`, cap `hidden_size` at 96, raise `feature_mask_rate` to 0.15) to any cluster with historical overfit_gap > 0.15 instead of only the three hand-picked ones. Expected impact: reduces memorization, lifts test precision consistency across the universe.

5. **Replace degenerate-metric rows in backtest report and investigate negative info ratios** — Stage 6. Bear-regime rows have Sortino=0, Calmar=inf (divide-by-zero, 15-25 trades). Add a guard in `src/evaluation/backtest.py` to mark rows with `n_trades < 30` or `max_dd < 0.5%` as "insufficient data" rather than emitting 5491× Calmar. Separately, every `info_ratio` is negative (-0.52 to -28.98) — investigate whether the benchmark return is computed correctly (annualized vs period), and whether the portfolio-return aggregation matches the benchmark timeframe. Expected impact: honest backtest numbers, clear signal when the portfolio underperforms the benchmark (likely the case today), and a path toward info-ratio-driven improvements.
